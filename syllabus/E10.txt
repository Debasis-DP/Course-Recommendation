<br/><b>OPTICAL SPECTROSCOPY:</b><br/> METHODS AND APPLICATION<br/>
<br/><b>PREREQUISITE:</b><br/>  Organic Chemistry
<br/>
<br/>The course will be a survey of major important instrumental analytical techniques; their principles, instrumentation, applications, merits and demerits will be discussed.  Time constraints may dictate how much of the materials and the number of topics discussed.
<br/>
<br/><b>  1.  Introduction:</b><br/> 
<br/>	1)	The analytical process
<br/>	2)	Figures of merits
<br/>	3)	Calibration methods and matrix effects
<br/>
<br/><b> II.  Spectroscopic methods:</b><br/>
<br/>1)	Noise and Analytical Figures of Merits
<br/>a)	Definitions
<br/><b>b)	Types of noise:</b><br/> 
<br/><b>c)	Signal-to-noise enhancement:</b><br/>  hardware and software techniques
<br/>
<br/>2)	Introduction to Spectroscopy
<br/>a)	Electromagnetic radiation
<br/> i) Wave-like properties
<br/>ii) Particle-like properties
<br/>b)	Atomic and molecular energy levels, absorption
<br/>and emission processes.
<br/>
<br/>3)	Instrumentation for Optical Spectroscopy
<br/>a)	Overview of instrumentation
<br/>b)	Radiation sources
<br/>c)	Wavelength selectors
<br/>d)	Sample holders
<br/>e)	Detectors
<br/>f)	Data display and manipulation
<br/><b>g)	Instrument designs:</b><br/> dispersive versus nondispersive,
<br/>	single versus double beam
<br/>
<br/>4)	Ultra-violet and Visible Absorption Spectroscopy
<br/>a)	Definitions
<br/>b)	Beer's Law and Quantitation
<br/>c)	Deviations and limitations to Beer's Law
<br/>d)	Instrumentation for UV-VIS spectroscopy
<br/>	 i) Components and designs
<br/>	ii) Actual commercial instruments
<br/>e)	Methods and applications of absorption spectroscopy
<br/> 
<br/>5)	Molecular Fluorescence, Phosphorescence and Chemiluminescence
<br/>a)	Principles of luminescence
<br/>b)	Instrumentation for fluorescence and phosphorescence
<br/>c)	Chemiluminescence
<br/>d)	Applications of luminescence techniques
<br/>
