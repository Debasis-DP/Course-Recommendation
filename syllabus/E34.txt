<br/>Experimental Techniques in Materials Engineering
<br/>----------------------------------------------------------------------------------
<br/>
<br/><b>Course Content:</b><br/>
<br/>
<br/>1. Introductory courses
<br/>– Introduction to Materials Engineering, Science, Engineering and Technology of Materials
<br/>
<br/>2. Basics and fundamentals
<br/>– Principles of Physical Metallurgy, Metallurgical Thermodynamics, Extractive Metallurgy, Phase transformations, Transport Phenomena, Mechanical Metallurgy (incl. creep, fatigue and fracture mechanics),
<br/>Physics of Materials, Characterisation of Materials, Powder Metallurgy, Refractories, Ceramics, Electronic, Magnetic and other properties
<br/>– Associated laboratories to improve understanding
<br/>
<br/>3.  Application aspects
<br/>– Environmental Degradation, Iron and Steel making, Processing of Materials - Forming, Joining and Casting, Computational techniques
<br/>– Adequate laboratory/demonstration classes 
<br/>
<br/>4. Case studies from the real world
<br/>– Defects and Failures in Manufacturing and Service, Metallurgical Plant Design
<br/>– Industrial Training and Lectures
<br/>
<br/>5. Capability for (independent) research
<br/>– Project work<br/><br/>
