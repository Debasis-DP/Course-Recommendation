<br/>Materials in medical science
<br/>-----------------------------------------------------
<br/>
<br/><b>Course Content:</b><br/>
<br/>UNIT-I
<br/>Properties of materials, Structure of solids, Introduction of biomaterials, Metallic implant
<br/><b>materials:</b><br/> Austenitic stainless steel, Co-based alloys, Titanium-based alloys and Dental alloys,
<br/>
<br/>
<br/>UNIT-II
<br/><b>Degradation processes on metallic surfaces, Ceramics implant materials:</b><br/> Glasses and Glassceramics,
<br/>Alumina, Zirconia and Calcium phosphate, Bioresorbable and bioactive ceramics,
<br/><b>Polymeric implant materials:</b><br/> Inert polymers, Natural polymers, Bioactive polymers,
<br/><b>Biodegradable polymers, Composite implant materials:</b><br/> Mechanics of composite, Different
<br/>composite biomaterials, Application of composite biomaterials
<br/>
<br/>UNIT-III
<br/>Structure-property relationships of biological materials, Tissue response to implant surface,
<br/><b>Softtissue replacement:</b><br/> Skin, Sutures, Maxillofacial implants and Blood interfacing implants,
<br/><b>Hard tissue replacement:</b><br/> Long bone repair-wires, pins, screws, fractures plates, intramedullary
<br/>devices, joint replacement-knee and hip joint, dental restorations and spinal implants.
<br/>
<br/>UNIT-IV
<br/><b>Characterization of biomaterials:</b><br/> Physical and physicochemical surface characterization<br/>Mechanical, Optical and Electrochemical characterization. Biocompatibility and Biological tests.
<br/>Infection and sterilization
