<br/>Science of nano-materials
<br/>------------------------------------------
<br/>
<br/><b>Course Content:</b><br/>
<br/>UNIT-I
<br/>Introduction to nanotechnology, definition, history. What makes the nanoscale so different from
<br/>the other length scales by considering the under pinning science (i.e.nanoscience) and some
<br/><b>key examples of nanotechnology; Properties in nanoscale:</b><br/> Extensive and Intensive properties,
<br/>change in physical properties like color, melting point, electrical, magnetic, and mechanical.
<br/>
<br/>UNIT-II
<br/>Quantum mechanical approach to explain the properties change in nanoscale. Theory of size
<br/>dependent melting point, effect of grain size and grain boundary on mechanical properties of
<br/>nanomaterial’s. 
<br/>
<br/>UNIT-III
<br/>Methods of synthesis of nanometerials fabrication-“Top-down” vs. “bottom-up”
<br/>approaches. A brief idea on synthesis of different nanomaterial’s. Theory of nucleation and
<br/>growth. Brief introduction to application of nanoparticles in catalysis, biotechnology, sensor etc.
<br/>
<br/>UNIT-IV
<br/>Characterization of nanoparticles by Scanning probe microscopes (Atomic Force Microscopy,
<br/>Scanning Tunneling Microscopy), Transmission Electron Microscopy, Scanning Electron
<br/>Microscopy.
<br/>
